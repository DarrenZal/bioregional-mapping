# Bridge note: RC LHC Phase 2 Program Guide → bioregional-mapping canon

## Context

The Regenerate Cascadia Landscape Hub Cultivator Phase 2 program runs April–July 2026 across nine landscape groups in Cascadia, with each group preparing for and hosting a bioregional mapping workshop in their community. The Program Guide is the authoritative document for the program; Darren is in Victoria's Landscape Group via SalishOakSeeds. This bridge note records source claims from the Program Guide that bear on bioregional-mapping canon. Disposition is `clarify` — the Program Guide articulates RC's framing of the practice; the bridge note records that articulation precisely without proposing canon changes on first pass.

This is the day-1 seed bridge note for the bioregional-mapping repo (created 2026-05-04 per `bioregional-mapping:ADR-0001`). It establishes the repo's bridge-note authoring pattern and registers RC Phase 2 as the primary near-term operational pressure source.

## Source claims

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §1 Program Overview] *Bioregional mapping is a practice and a way of seeing, not just a one-time event.* RC explicitly frames the workshop not as a high-stakes single output but as the start of a conversation, a rhythm of gathering, building a "whole system" tone through diverse-stakeholder convening. This frames mapping as ongoing coordination shape, not a deliverable type — which aligns with this repo's framing of mapping as multi-layer practice.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §1 Program Overview] *The bioregional mapping process holds the evolving Story of your Place — something that unfolds over time in cycles and rhythms and that people can continue to add to and locate themselves within.* Story of Place is named as the central, persistent output of the practice; it is explicitly NOT a single high-stakes artifact but a Field-like coordination substrate that participants and future stewards inherit. This corresponds canonically to a Field manifestation in Spore terms (Field-as-Story-of-Place specialization, surfaced as candidate at `bioregional-mapping.project-vision` §How upstream primitives specialize).

**C3** [confidence: high] [anchor: §1 Learning Objectives] *RC's mapping layer rubric is "physical, ecological, social, cultural, economic, and regenerative."* This is a six-layer set distinct from but compatible with bioregional-mapping's "relational, cultural, ecological, economic, governance, geospatial" rubric. The two sets converge on layer-multiplicity-as-canonical and diverge on specific layer naming — RC includes "regenerative" as a distinct layer where bioregional-mapping treats regeneration as a cross-cutting orientation. Layer naming is provisional; layer multiplicity is canonical.

**C4** [confidence: high] [anchor: §3 Section 1 + §1] *Bioregional mapping practice is rooted in Indigenous ways of being and knowing, breaks the pattern of seeing through artificial boundaries and categories, and demonstrates organic / interdependent / ecosystem approach over siloed / sectoral / project-based approach.* RC names Indigenous-led methodological grounding as constitutive (not optional), aligns with this repo's framing of cultural-layer-as-membrane carrying FPIC + sovereign-territory load-bearing constraints, and aligns with BioKulture engineering blue paper (Zenodo 10.5281/zenodo.16656790) parallel framing.

**C5** [confidence: high] [anchor: §4 Outcomes 1 + 2] *Two of four primary MVP outcomes are: (1) demonstrate whole-system approach via diverse participants; (2) workshop outputs create an initial Story of Place that every participant can place themselves within.* These outcomes operationalize Story-of-Place-as-Field manifestation (from C2) and the multi-stakeholder joint-commitment shape of the workshop (which corresponds to spore:ADR-0050 joint-commitment primitive, paradigm-case-load-bearing per `bioregional-mapping.project-vision` §Workshop-as-Joint-Commitment).

**C6** [confidence: high] [anchor: §5 Core Resources] *RC's Bioregional Mapping Field Guide for Landscape Stewards (target April 10, 2026) is a "training-the-trainers manual covering the philosophy of bioregional mapping, step-by-step workshop facilitation, and documentation methods."* The Field Guide is explicitly named as the primary methodological reference for Phase 2 — a Reproduction artifact in Spore terms, transmitting practice across the LHC cohort. This corresponds to `bioregional-mapping.project-vision` §"Field Guide as Reproduction artifact" candidate specialization.

**C7** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §1 Program Overview] *Phase 2 workshop is positioned as a "probe" — taking action not to be experts at it, but to discover together and draw meaning from those discoveries to inform next action.* This frames the workshop epistemically: practice generates evidence + signals (Spore primitives) about what works at the layer level, feeding into Phase 3 regeneration-strategy co-design. The probe framing is consistent with iterative, learning-by-doing posture; explicit caveat against expert-level expectations.

**C8** [confidence: high] [anchor: §4 Workshop Approach] *Even 10 participants succeed if they represent different areas within the landscape ecosystem.* Diversity-of-stakeholder-types is the success criterion, not participant count. This formalizes a quality-not-quantity heuristic for joint-commitment shape — adjacent to spore.federation-encounter pattern (federation-to-federation encounters require representational-adequacy across constituent holons, not headcount).

**C9** [confidence: high] [anchor: §6 Roles] *Two-to-three Landscape Stewards per Landscape Group serve as bridges between LHC programming and broader Landscape Group.* Stewards operationalize the practitioner role at the workshop-prep layer; the bridge-shape (programmatic ↔ community) is itself a coordination primitive operating at the workshop's pre-commitment stage.

**C10** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §8 Year Ahead] *September 2026 Turtle Island Bioregional Congress (TIBC) in Vernonia, Oregon is a landmark gathering for bioregional practitioners across the continent — an extraordinary opportunity for Landscape Groups to share Story of Place artifacts.* TIBC is positioned as cross-bioregional-federation gathering where Story-of-Place artifacts cross out of their local bioregion and into wider practitioner field — a federation-encounter event in Spore terms (spore:ADR-0068 federation-encounter composition-pattern is load-bearing here).
