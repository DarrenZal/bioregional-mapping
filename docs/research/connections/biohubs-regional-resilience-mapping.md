# BioHubs: A Pathway to Regional Resilience → bioregional-mapping

## Context

*BioHubs: A Pathway to Regional Resilience* (Metabolic et al., May 2026; lead author Eva Gladek) maps **152 BioHub-like initiatives across 44 countries** through three filtering criteria, drawing on web/AI-assisted search and practitioner referrals plus 30+ interviews. From a mapping standpoint the whitepaper is itself a **mapping artifact** — a qualified repository snapshot of a field — and it surfaces several scale and method insights directly relevant to bioregional-mapping canon.

Disposition is `clarify existing term`: the whitepaper articulates field-level mapping patterns (site-vs-reach scale, the multi-domain rubric, Story of Place as method) that align with this repo's framing of mapping as multi-layer practice, without proposing canon changes on first pass. Concepts are drawn only from the frozen vocabulary.

## Claim Register

**C1** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Project Repository: At a Glance · orn:biohubs.whitepaper:repository]
*The repository of 152 initiatives across 44 countries is an explicitly partial, English-skewed snapshot (Europe/North America over-represented; coastal/aquatic hubs notably absent), surfaced through three filtering criteria.* This is a `tooling-map`-adjacent artifact: an indexed field survey with documented coverage gaps. The methodological honesty about skew is itself a precedent for how this repo should caveat its own mapping outputs.

**C2** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Sites & Their Reach]
*Site footprint and area of influence are distinct mapping quantities: across 59 sized projects the median site is ~44 ha (mean 285 ha), and 12 hubs that record a stewardship/influence area report it averaging 21× (up to 150×+) the owned site — e.g. Sinal do Vale operates a 173-ha site across a 200,000-ha watershed.* The site-vs-reach gap is a load-bearing mapping distinction at the `watershed`/`bioregion` scale: the polygon a hub owns is not the polygon it stewards.

**C3** [confidence: high] [anchor: §BioHub Design Framework · §Ingredients]
*The seven "ingredients" (Land & Ecology; Food & Material Flows; Knowledge & Learning; Culture & Social Fabric; Governance & Stewardship; Finance & Enterprise; Human Health & Inner Development) compose a multi-domain reading of a place.* This is a near-parallel to this repo's six-layer rubric (relational/cultural/ecological/economic/governance/geospatial) — an external corroboration that mapping a hub/region means holding multiple domains as distinct `layer`s rather than a single map.

**C4** [confidence: high] [anchor: §Finding Your Focal Point]
*The Regenesis Group's "Story of Place" — reading a place as the sum of its ecological, cultural, and economic energies, uncovering its "vocation" before intervening — is presented as the foundational pre-mapping assessment.* This directly affirms `story-of-place` as both method and artifact in this repo: deep regional assessment precedes and grounds any focal intervention.

**C5** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §Recurring Patterns of Success]
*A recurring success pattern is long-horizon `stewardship` and deep rootedness in place — a ~five-year threshold (echoed by Bill Reed of Regenesis) before results become visible enough to attract consistent support.* For mapping `practitioner`s this sets a realistic time-base: mapping is the start of a multi-year stewardship rhythm, not a one-time deliverable — consistent with this repo's framing of mapping as ongoing `field` practice.

**C6** [confidence: medium] [anchor: §The Peri-Urban Sweet Spot]
*Under a proximity classification, 47% of hubs are peri-urban, 41% rural/remote, 12% urban — challenging the field's default rural orientation.* A geospatial-`layer` finding worth carrying into this repo's site-classification scheme: proximity/commute-access is a distinct axis from population density or land character.

## Bridge Fit

- The 152-initiative repository ↔ `tooling-map` / field-survey artifact form, with explicit coverage caveats.
- Site-vs-reach (21×) ↔ `watershed`/`bioregion` scale distinction between owned and stewarded extent.
- Seven ingredients ↔ the six-layer rubric (`layer`): external corroboration of multi-domain mapping.
- Story of Place ↔ `story-of-place` as method + artifact.
